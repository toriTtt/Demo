High-Fidelity Voice Conversion Based on Mamba-2 Dynamic Aggregation and Residual Vector Quantization

Li Tian· Ning Chen*· Xinran Wang· Hongqing Zhu· Zhiying Zhu

East China University of Science and Technology, Shanghai, 200237, China

*Corresponding author

Overview

Abstract

Effective disentanglement between speaker identity and linguistic content is important to ensure the fidelity of the converted speech. The introduction of the self-supervised learning (SSL) model i.e. HuBERT, helps to enhance the performance of AdaptVC model. However, the adapter in it aggregates the hierarchical embeddings extracted by HuBERT with static and time-invariant weights, failing to capture the continuously varying acoustic characteristics in the speech and thus influencing the fidelity of the acoustic speech. Although single-stage vector quantization (VQ) helps to suppress speaker leakage to some extent, it may not make a good balance between feature disentanglement and acoustic detail preservation, leading to over-smoothed representations. To address these problems and enhance the fidelity of the converted speech, first, a Mamba-2-based dynamic adapter is constructed to generate temporally-conditioned frame-level weights and adaptively emphasize the embeddings extracted by different HuBERT layers based on acoustic context. Second, a Residual VQ (RVQ) is constructed to enforce hierarchical disentanglement across multiple quantization levels while retaining fine-grained acoustic contexts. Third, the Production Quality (PQ) is introduced in validation stage as model selection criterion to make the chosen model reflect human perceptual quality better. Experimental results demonstrate that the proposed model outperforms various competitive models including AdapterVC in terms of various objective and subjective evaluation metrics, and each of the above modifications helps to enhance the performance of the proposed model.
